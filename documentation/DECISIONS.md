## DECISION 1: 3-Month Timeline (Jan 19, 2026)

**Question:** Fast launch (1 week) vs bulletproof build (3 months)?

**Options Considered:**
- A: 1 week launch, weak claims, high risk
- B: 3 months, academic validation, undeniable
- C: 1 month middle path

**Decision:** OPTION B - 3 months to bulletproof

**Rationale:**
- Rim has university connections for validation
- Fighting corruption requires unassailable credibility
- No room for error when making fraud claims
- Better to be right than first

**Impact:** Launch moves to April 2026

---

## DECISION 2: Academic Validation First (Jan 19, 2026)

**Question:** Build tool first or get validation first?

**Decision:** Build → Validate → Launch (not Build → Launch → Validate)

**Rationale:**
- Professors won't validate vaporware
- Need working prototype to review
- Validation before launch = credibility armor
- Can incorporate feedback before going public

**Impact:** Week 5-8 dedicated to professor reviews

---

## DECISION 3: Measured Claims Strategy (Jan 19, 2026)

**Question:** What language do we use about findings?

**Decision:** "Fraud indicators" NOT "proven fraud"

**Approved Language:**
- ✅ "Exhibits multiple fraud indicators"
- ✅ "Patterns consistent with procurement irregularities"
- ✅ "Warrants investigation by authorities"

**Forbidden Language:**
- ❌ "This IS fraud"
- ❌ "We caught $X billion in corruption"
- ❌ "These vendors are criminals"

**Rationale:** Legal defensibility, academic standards, avoid litigation

---

## DECISION 4: Data Quality Over Quantity (Jan 19, 2026)

**Question:** Scrape 50,000 messy contracts or 10,000 clean ones?

**Decision:** 10,000 high-quality contracts with full metadata

**Rationale:**
- Quality beats quantity for academic credibility
- Full metadata (amendments, bid type) enables better detection
- 10,000 is statistically significant
- Easier to verify and defend

**Impact:** Focus on data cleaning and validation

---

## DECISION 5: Open Source Everything (Jan 19, 2026)

**Question:** Keep methodology proprietary or open source?

**Decision:** Full transparency - code, data, methodology all public

**Rationale:**
- "Prove us wrong" strategy requires transparency
- Academic credibility demands reproducibility
- Journalists need to verify findings
- Aligns with "Sunlight is the best disinfectant" mission

**Impact:** GitHub repository, public documentation, downloadable datasets

---

## DECISION 6: Legal Framework Grounding (Jan 19, 2026)

**Question:** Just detect patterns or tie to specific laws?

**Decision:** Every detection method maps to specific legal framework

**Requirements:**
- Cite relevant statutes (False Claims Act, Anti-Kickback, etc.)
- Reference actual DOJ prosecution cases
- Document legal thresholds
- Get law professor review if possible

**Rationale:** Transforms from "interesting patterns" to "legally-grounded evidence"

## DECISION 7: Week 1 Target: 1,000 Contracts (Jan 19, 2026)

**Question:** How many contracts to scrape Week 1?

**Decision:** 1,000 contracts with quality validation

**Rationale:**
- 10x current dataset (92)
- Enough for meaningful analysis
- Manageable for quality checking
- Foundation for professor review
- Builds toward 10,000 final target

**Impact:** Enhanced scraper with pagination needed immediately

## DECISION 8: Professor Outreach Timing (Jan 19, 2026)

**Question:** When to approach professors?

**Decision:** Week 2 (after 1,000 contracts analyzed)

**Rationale:**

- Too late = no time to incorporate feedback

**Targets:**

2. Political Science prof (legal/corruption framework)
