# ABSTRACT

Government procurement fraud costs taxpayers hundreds of billions annually, yet traditional auditing methods detect only a small fraction of cases. We present SUNLIGHT, a multi-source fraud detection system combining price analysis, political donation tracking, and legal framework alignment.

Our methodology integrates data from USAspending.gov and OpenSecrets.org using statistical baseline comparisons adjusted for contract size and type. We employ a three-tier classification (RED/YELLOW/GREEN) based on DOJ prosecution thresholds.

We validate our approach on 477 federal contracts totaling $1.2 billion. Our conservative methodology identifies one contract meeting investigation-worthy thresholds. All methodology, code, and data sources are open source for independent verification.

**Keywords:** government procurement, fraud detection, statistical anomaly, transparency, accountability
